Linzer Torte Recipe

Description

Linzer Torte is a classic German/Austrian pastry made with a buttery nut-based crust, filled with tart fruit jam, and finished with a traditional lattice top.

It's rich, slightly spiced, and perfectly balanced between sweet and tangy.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups finely ground almonds
  • 1 ¼ cups white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cinammon
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1 ⅛ cups unsalted butter
  • 4 egg yolks, divided
  • 6 tablespoons red currant jam

Steps

  1. Place flour, ground almonds, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon, and cloves on a clean work surface. Add butter and 2 egg yolks; knead quickly to form a smooth dough. Wrap dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 1 hour.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 10-inch springform pan with remaining 1 tablespoon butter.
  3. Press 2/3 of the dough evenly in the pan so that the entire bottom is covered. Spread red currant jam on top. Divide remaining dough into several pieces and roll into 1/3-inch strands between your fingertips. Arrange the strips over the jam in a neat lattice pattern. Place the last strip along the sides of the pan to form a rim.
  4. Beat remaining 2 egg yolks and brush over the lattice and rim, taking care not to splash onto the jam.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 45 minutes. Watch carefully towards the end of the baking time to make sure the torte does not turn too dark. Let cool and remove gently from the pan.

Enjoy your Linzer Torte!
